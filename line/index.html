1976
Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Rollan Wayne found Apple Computer, Inc.
The apple 1 goes on sale, which was the first personal computer manufactured and sold by Apple. It was designed by Steve Wozniak and was sold as a DIY kit, meaning buyers had to assemble the computer themselves.

1977
The company launches the Apple II.
The first mass-marketed personal computer.
It featured a color display, built-in keyboard, BASIC programming language, graphical user interface, and modular design for easy memory expansion. In addition, it had a wide variety of ports and connectors for peripherals. It was an important foundation for future technological innovations.

1980
Apple Inc. becomes a public company.
With an initial public offering of shares, which allowed the company to raise $110 million dollars. This was an important event in the company's history and allowed the company to raise the necessary capital to finance its growth and development.

1983
Apple introduces Lisa, the first computer with a mouse.
Lisa had an advanced graphical user interface and a mouse for interaction with the operating system. Lisa was the first commercialized personal computer with these features.

1984
Apple introduces the Macintosh.
The Macintosh was Apple's first personal computer to feature a graphical user interface, meaning that users could interact with the computer using a mouse and a display with icons and windows.

1985 - 1997
Jobs is fired from Apple.
At the end of the year, Jobs forms Next Inc, of high-end computers for universities. In 1997 Apple bought NeXT for $430 million. Jobs returns as Apple's interim CEO and chairman. A year later Apple released the iMac, which puts the monitor and processor in a single unit. Apple returns to profitability.

2002
Apple introduces the iPod and iTunes.
Apple launches the iPod a portable MP3 player that revolutionizes the way you listen to music, for this reason Apple also launches the iTunes Store, giving people an easy way to buy music online.

2007
Apple introduces the iPhone.
It was one of the first smartphones, and for the first time in history, it did not have a keyboard, but a 3.5-inch touch screen. Crowds queue up at stores to buy.

2010
Apple introduces the iPad.
A touchscreen tablet PC that ushers in a new genre of mobile computing. It was an instant success, selling more than 14.8 million units in its first year. It was the first device of its kind to be widely accepted by the market and helped establish a new category of mobile devices: tablets.
